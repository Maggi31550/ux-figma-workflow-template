# Structured Rejection Reasons With Corrective Guidance Reduce Repeat-Error Resubmissions

**Category:** Principle

**Source:** OCPB DataSure research, 2026-05-12

**2-Year Check:** The causal chain is permanent: vague rejection messages produce vague corrections, which produce further rejections. This dynamic exists in every review workflow regardless of technology or context and will not resolve on its own.

When a reviewer rejects a submission with only a generic reason (e.g. "ขาดเอกสาร"), applicants resubmit with the same errors because they cannot identify what specifically to correct. A structured rejection system — a dropdown of categorised reason codes plus a mandatory guidance note mapped to each reason — gives the applicant a checklist of corrections rather than a diagnosis they must interpret. This converts a review workflow bottleneck into a self-service correction loop.

**Applies to:** Any submission-review workflow: grant applications, regulatory filings, content moderation appeals, certification systems, permit renewals.
