# A Pre-flight Checklist Before the Form Reduces Mid-Flow Abandonment

**Category:** Pattern

**Source:** OCPB DataSure research, 2026-05-12

**2-Year Check:** The barrier is not digital literacy — it is the surprise of encountering document requirements mid-form. This cognitive friction is structural and will not change with improved internet access or device quality. GOV.UK's "one thing per page" research predates this project by a decade and the pattern still holds.

SME operators abandon multi-step government forms when they encounter a document requirement they cannot satisfy mid-session. Showing a complete "what you will need" checklist before the first input field — including specific file formats, agency reference numbers, and estimated time — allows users to gather materials first and return prepared, reducing mid-flow drop-off. This applies regardless of whether "Save and return later" is available, because the checklist prevents entry of an uncompletable session entirely.

**Applies to:** Any multi-step government or compliance form requiring third-party documents (tax IDs, regulatory certificates, uploaded files). E-commerce seller onboarding, permit applications, professional licence submissions.
